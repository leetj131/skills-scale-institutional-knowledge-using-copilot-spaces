# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers are responsible for the end-to-end user experience of OctoAcme products. They translate product requirements into intuitive designs, ensuring usability and accessibility standards are met across all deliverables.

### Responsibilities
- Create user flows, wireframes, and high-fidelity prototypes
- Conduct usability research and accessibility audits
- Define and maintain design standards and component libraries
- Collaborate with Product Managers to validate requirements against user needs
- Partner with Developers to ensure accurate implementation of designs
- Review front-end changes and provide design sign-off before release

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and visually consistent user experiences
- Reduce friction in user journeys and minimize support escalations driven by UX issues
- Establish shared design language across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critiques with Product Managers and Developers
- Prototypes and annotated mockups shared via design tooling (e.g., Figma)
- Accessibility and usability findings documented and tracked in the project board

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers own all documentation deliverables for the project. They ensure that users, developers, and operators have accurate, up-to-date materials to understand and use OctoAcme products effectively.

### Responsibilities
- Author, maintain, and publish end-user, developer, and process documentation
- Collaborate with Developers to document APIs, configuration, and technical guides
- Partner with Product Managers and Project Managers to produce release notes and handover materials
- Review and update existing documentation for accuracy after each release
- Identify and close documentation gaps flagged during retrospectives

### Goals
- Ensure documentation is accurate, complete, and published in time for each release
- Reduce support escalations attributable to inadequate or missing documentation
- Maintain a single source of truth for all product and process docs

### Typical Communication
- Documentation review cycles with Developers and Product Managers before release
- Changelog and release notes drafts shared with Project Manager for approval
- Async collaboration via pull requests and doc review comments

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
The QA Lead owns the quality assurance strategy and coordinates all testing activities. They work with the team to define acceptance criteria, establish test plans, and confirm release readiness.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the overall test strategy (manual and automated)
- Coordinate test planning, execution, and defect triage across the team
- Align acceptance criteria with Product Managers and Developers
- Gate release readiness by verifying all critical test scenarios pass
- Drive improvements to test coverage and automation infrastructure
- Report quality metrics and defect trends to the Project Manager

### Goals
- Prevent regressions and ensure every release meets defined acceptance criteria
- Increase automated test coverage to reduce manual testing bottlenecks
- Provide clear and objective release-readiness signals to the team

### Typical Communication
- Test plan reviews during sprint planning with Project Manager and Developers
- Daily defect triage and status updates during active testing cycles
- Release readiness sign-off communicated to Project Manager and Product Manager

---

## Support Lead

### Role Summary
The Support Lead represents operational and customer support needs during project planning and delivery. They serve as the bridge between end users, support teams, and the product/project organization, ensuring smooth release handoffs and effective feedback loops.

### Responsibilities
- Participate in release planning to assess support readiness and training needs
- Document escalation paths, runbooks, and support handoff materials for each release
- Relay customer and user feedback to Product Managers and Project Managers
- Coordinate with QA Lead and Developers on known issues and workarounds
- Define and maintain SLAs and escalation criteria for production incidents
- Ensure support staff are trained and ready before new features go live

### Goals
- Minimize customer-impacting incidents after release
- Shorten time-to-resolution for support escalations
- Provide actionable user feedback to improve product quality and roadmap decisions

### Typical Communication
- Pre-release readiness reviews with Project Manager and QA Lead
- Post-release incident and feedback summaries shared with Product Manager
- Escalation notifications and handoff documentation distributed to support team

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather, analyze, and document business and stakeholder requirements. They act as the connective tissue between stakeholders, Product Managers, and the delivery team, ensuring that scope is clearly understood and agreed upon before and during delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document requirements through stakeholder interviews and workshops
- Translate business needs into clear, actionable user stories and acceptance criteria
- Identify and resolve ambiguous, conflicting, or incomplete requirements
- Assess the impact of scope changes and communicate trade-offs to Project Manager and Product Manager
- Maintain requirement traceability from stakeholder need to delivered feature
- Validate delivered features against documented requirements

### Goals
- Eliminate ambiguity in requirements before development begins
- Ensure alignment between stakeholder expectations and delivered outcomes
- Reduce rework caused by misunderstood or incomplete requirements

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews during initiation and planning
- User story and acceptance criteria reviews with Product Manager and Developers
- Impact and scope change reports shared with Project Manager
